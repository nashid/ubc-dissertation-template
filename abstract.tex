\chapter{Abstract}

Replace this text with your abstract.  A common and effective
structure: one short paragraph motivating the problem and stating the
gap in existing work; one or two paragraphs summarizing each of your
contributions and the key results; and a closing paragraph stating the
overall significance of the dissertation.

For a manuscript-based dissertation, a sentence or two per research
chapter works well: ``First, we present \ldots\ Second, we develop
\ldots\ Finally, we conduct \ldots''  End by tying the contributions
back to the thesis statement.
